- In 2017, TikTok‚Äôs parent company, ByteDance, scraped short-form videos, usernames, profile pictures, and profile descriptions from Instagram, Snapchat, and other sources and then uploaded them ‚Äî without users‚Äô knowledge or consent ‚Äî to Flipagram, a TikTok predecessor, according to four former employees of the company.
- BuzzFeed News spoke with the four former ByteDance employees, all of whom worked on Flipagram (later renamed Vigo Video), and viewed internal documents that indicate the scraping was run by an engineering team in China and began soon after ByteDance acquired Flipagram in January 2017. The former employees described the project as one of several ‚Äúgrowth hacks‚Äù ‚Äî including the manipulation of like and video view statistics ‚Äî employed by the company. The former employees spoke to BuzzFeed News under the condition of anonymity because they feared retribution from ByteDance.
- The former employees do not know when the scraping they say they were aware of stopped. Two of them say that the scraped content was used to train ByteDance‚Äôs powerful ‚ÄúFor You‚Äù personalization algorithm on US-based content so that it would better reflect the preferences of US users. Today, the ‚ÄúFor You‚Äù algorithm powers both TikTok and its Chinese equivalent, Douyin. (Disclosure: In a previous life, I held policy positions at Facebook and Spotify.)
- BuzzFeed News sent ByteDance a comprehensive list of the allegations we intended to print in this article as well as a detailed set of questions, including if data sets from Flipagram were ever used to train the ‚ÄúFor You‚Äù algorithm that powers TikTok today or to train any other algorithms currently in use by ByteDance.
- In response, ByteDance spokesperson Jennifer Banks wrote back two sentences: ‚ÄúByteDance acquired Flipagram in 2017 and operated it, and subsequently Vigo, for a short time. Flipagram and Vigo ceased operations years ago and aren't connected to any current ByteDance products.‚Äù

- The documents reviewed by BuzzFeed News include explicit references to scraped content and the use of fake accounts. In one document, an employee lays out the reasons that the company used ‚Äúfake accounts‚Äù and scraped content; among them were that the accounts could be used to test which content performed best on the platform, and that current users could mimic the scraped content to improve their own popularity. A third document lists account scraping as an ‚ÄúOKR‚Äù (‚Äòobjective and key result‚Äô) for an engineering team in China.
- According to the documents, ByteDance began copying content from some of its China-focused short-form video apps and uploading it to Flipagram through fake accounts in early 2017. - In mid-2017, according to the four former employees, ByteDance began scraping and reuploading content from the US. - One former employee portrayed ByteDance‚Äôs growth tactics as a symptom of a larger, industry-wide obsession with growth at any cost.
